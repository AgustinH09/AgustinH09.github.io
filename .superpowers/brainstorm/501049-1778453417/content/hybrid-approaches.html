Achieving Both: Accessibility + Technical Depth

Here are two different ways we can blend a highly readable, accessible experience with a hardcore technical aesthetic.

1

The "Modern IDE" Layout

Instead of a raw Linux TTY terminal, we structure the site like a modern code editor (like VS Code or Zed).

How it works: The sidebar acts as a "File Explorer", the main content area shows your projects and blog in highly readable, syntax-highlighted "files", and there is an interactive terminal panel at the bottom. It's immediately usable by anyone with a mouse, but screams "Systems Engineer".

2

The "Drop to Shell" Toggle (Dual Mode)

The site has two distinct faces that you can toggle instantly.

How it works: By default, it loads a stunning, minimalist, highly-readable portfolio (inspired by your friend's site, but with your dark Tokyo aesthetic). However, there's a persistent command prompt or a "GUI/CLI" toggle switch. Hitting it strips away the styling and drops the user into a full-screen, functional terminal to explore the exact same content.
